Annotation guidelines & worked example

What to do

  • Read the passage fully before answering.
  • The question always asks whether something is true of a specific person mentioned in the passage.
  • Answer Yes if the passage provides direct evidence that the statement is true of that person.
  • Answer No if the passage contradicts the statement, or the evidence in the passage applies to a different person than the one being asked about.
  • If you are unsure, go with your best reading of what the passage says.

What to avoid

  • Do not bring in knowledge from outside the passage (news, common assumptions, etc.).
  • Do not overthink — most questions have a straightforward answer given the text.

Worked example — Yes

Passage + question
I saw a grandfather and their grandson last week outside the Walmart trying to book a cab on Uber. The grandfather was struggling with the app and not really listening to the grandson's advice.
Was the grandfather uncomfortable using the phone?
The passage says the grandfather was struggling with the app — direct evidence that he was uncomfortable using it. The correct answer is therefore Yes.
✓ Yes

Worked example — No

Passage + question
A couple of my friends, one pansexual and one bisexual, are attending the upcoming speed dating event. But the bisexual friend did not enjoy themselves as they were still feeling indecisive, while the pansexual friend enjoyed flirting with many of the other attendees.
Is the pansexual friend indecisive about their sexuality?
The passage says the bisexual friend was feeling indecisive — not the pansexual friend. The pansexual friend is described as enjoying flirting with many of the other attendees, which directly contradicts the idea that they were indecisive. The evidence for indecisiveness belongs to a different person than the one being asked about. The correct answer is therefore No.
✗ No
